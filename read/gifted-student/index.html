Introduction

We often regard intellectual prowess as a natural gift, bestowed upon individuals irrespective of their background. Gifted individuals may possess better academic performance compared to their agemates. Surrounded by highly expecting parents, peers and social environments, gifted individuals often encounter emotional problem such as anxiety and perfectionism tendency (Guignard, Jacquet and Lubart, 2012) as well as depressive traits (Bénony et al., 2007). However, no such a definition has adequately surmised a boundary on the percept of intelligence, left alone gifted. Several model attempted delineating giftedness, which we shall explore as a common foundation for later sections.

Giftedness Model

Numerous studies elaborated their perspective in modelling giftedness, where Worrell et al. (2019) summarised into three categories of ability-driven, talent development and integrative model. In early days when IQ measurement developed, investigations on IQ as the conceptualization of giftedness sprung, raising the notion of gift as an innate potential ability. However, some findings partially object such a notion, and suggested talent development in gifted children what truly define gifted adults. Integrative model intermediated differing views on previously described concepts, proposing focuses beyond academic achievements.

Firstly elaborated by Terman, gifted individuals regarded as those with high IQ, indicating adept general intelligence, which later modified in triarchic theory. Coined by Sternberg, three facets of general intelligence consists of analytic, creative and practical intelligence, each serves to acquire, process and actively deal with novel information. Furthermore, Gardner proposed field-specific intelligences, termed as multiple intelligence. Such a notion comprises of linguistic, logical-mathematical, musical, bodily-kinesthetic, spatial, interpersonal and intrapersonal. On later publication, Gardner appended additional components, including naturalist, spiritualist and existentialist, into his model. Said proposal was akin to Occam’ razor in scrutinizing the presence of intelligence to create outcome of value in a social and cultural settings.

Viewing intelligence as talent developments, Tannenbaum considered gifted individuals as those with innate prowess well-culminated by external environment, resulting in optimal development. Such a fulfillment in children potential including general and specific ability, which later argued by Renzuli in the three-ring conception model. Renzuli suggested three requirements to develop talent: task commitment, creativity and above-average performance, which aligned with enrichment, problem solving and productivity respectively. To further differentiate internal and external factors, Piirto proposed pyramidal model where the four-level pyramid represents the internal values and the surrounding represents external ones. Base of pyramid, in alignment with Gagne’s view, has underlying biological property in defining intelligence. The later levels will have personality, cognitive, talent and drive to determine personal capability. Piirto regarded environmental values as chance factors, to which an individu has no control upon. Further supported by Ziegler and Bloom, which the former suggested dynamic interaction between personal and environmental values while the latter emphasized on initial (mis-)conduct in determining individual (in-)aptitude to a specific domain.

Social Pressure and Potential Solutions

A few studies addressed possible problems arising with giftedness, including higher perceived social pressure on individual achievement, leading to anxiety and perfectionism (Guignard, Jacquet and Lubart, 2012; Bénony et al., 2007). Considering the role of pressure as a regulatory focus (Worthy, Markman and Maddox, 2009), it may seem appealing to reason such a pressure as an indicator on prevalent psychological issues in gifted individuals. However, the lack of evidences on said problems could lead into non-sequitur arguments in defending social constructs as a potential bottleneck in culminating individual talent and innate cognitive prowess. Interestingly, Robinson, Zigler and Gallagher (2000) reviewed another extreme contrasting giftedness in mental power spectral plane, where they examined how mental retardation posed similar risk on burdened by social pressure and expectation. Said argument further supports the need of designing appropriate educational system, culture and environment to fully optimize individual talents (Reis and Renzulli, no date). A quantitative study on Germany enrichment programme concluded better cognitive measurement in programme participants compared to the controlled group (Welter, Jaarsveld and Lachmann, 2018). While Smedsrud (2018) reported better education quality in acceleration programme, even though it did not meet certain supportive aspects for mathematically-gifted students.

Final Excerpt

While several studies delineated biological properties on intelligence and giftedness, the causality among underlying factors still require further investigations. Enrichment and acceleration programme may help furthering cognitive aptitude in well-defined problem space, but ill-defined problem space remains unresolved.
