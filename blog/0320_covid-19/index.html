Preamble

The increasing numbers of publicly available data on COVID-19 infection enabled a few computational approaches to emerge. Previously presented in YouTube, where Grant Sanderson proposed https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kas0tIxDvrg&t=28s. Another explanation in The Washington Post also greatly appealed computational modelling on https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/world/corona-simulator/. Both regard social dynamic as a random event, where population spatial movement depicted as either permutation changes or Brownian motion. In this simulation, I wish to use network modelling as a layer of abstraction in explaining population movement and COVID-19 growth rate. Furthermore, by applying edges deletion from such a network, we may visualize how social distancing and self-quarantine may help reducing the viral transmission. In creating such a network, Watts-Strogatz small world model will represent general population and Markov Chain will estimate the probability of having infection.
